Application of DAG

Posted on 2016-07-12   |   In Application

This blog summarizes several applications of Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) model.

Risk assessment for hypertension and hypertension complications incidences using a Bayesian network

Contributions: This paper uses BN network to model the hypertension and hypertension complications incidences. First Cox regression method as well as domain knowledge are used to select variables. Then the data are discretized and sub-sampled to build the network. In the end several validation methods are implemented including domain knowledge, the probability inference and the accuracy, sensitivity, AUC. The contributions are that it implements the BN to this problem…

Structure learning:

  1. Constraint-based algorithms: have lower time complexity and better scalability
  2. Score-based algorithms: more accurate and stable
  3. This paper use Max-min Hill-Climbing (MMHC) algorithm, which combines constrain-based and score-based algorithms. It first use a conditional independency test to find a set of adjacent nodes for each node (constraint-based) and then performs a standard hill-climbing search to orient the edges between nodes (score-based).

The Domain Knowledge of SSI(Clinical Guideline)

Posted on 2016-07-12   |   In Application

This is the summary of the domain Knowledge of SSI. Domain knowledge is kind of important when you want to validate your result, improve the performance of your model or be more professional on your research.

What’s does this guideline say?

This is a guideline developed by a multi-professional and lay working group (the Guideline Development Group or GDG) convened by the National Collaborating Centre for Women’s and Children’s Health (NCC-WCH). It mainly tells these:

  1. What is SSI?
  2. Why it’s important to study it(Also see Health economics in section 5)?
  3. Recommendation: For health-care. For research.
  4. Process and tips for different phases(preoperative, intraoperative, postoperative)
  5. Some existing knowledge such as some risk factors can actually affect SSI.

If you want to publish a paper

Posted on 2016-07-12   |   In Writing

Lessons from reviews:

  1. Topics on irregular sampled clinical and time-series data can be promising.
  2. Some questions people may concerns theoretically: is your estimator unbiased? Low variances? Will your algorithm converges(From Prof Yang’s question and a formula in the paper about compressive sensing and SoV)?
  3. Good and convincing evaluations of the method is vital. That’s what Atlas’s points of not caring too much about the values, but caring the methods of showing and explaining your results, especially there exists some uncommon results.
  4. A clear line. People will read your experiments with your motivation. It’s important to be consistent.
  5. Grammar and sentences.

Model Selection

Posted on 2016-07-12   |   In Theory

Model selection methods are used when you have a set of candidate models or your models have some undicided parameters. In this case, you need to use the model selection mothod. Most of the contents are from wiki. But more contents will be added after reading some to-do list.

Model selection is a task of selecting a statistical model from a set of candidate models given data. Usually, a good model balances the goodness of fit and the model complexity(means avoiding overfitting). I get contacted with AIC, BIC, MDL and Cross Validation at this time point.
